Shiver Me Timbers

Some pirate phrases for you old salts.

Dead Men Tell No Tales

An expression that means dead people will not betray any secrets. Used as a threat to kill someone, or a way of saying there were no survivors.

Batten Down the Hatches

From the practice aboard a ship of sealing hatches to prevent water getting below-decks in a storm by using covers secured by strips of material, called battens, firmly attached to the frame of the hatch opening.

Davy Jones' Locker

Graveyard at the bottom of the sea for those killed or drowned. It is used as a euphemism for drowning or shipwrecks in which the sailors' and ships' remains are consigned to the depths of the ocean (to be sent to Davy Jones' Locker).

Hornswaggle

To swindle something, usually money, out of someone else. Though the origins of this word are unknown, its first recorded use appears to be in 1829.

Marooned

To be abandoned with no food, drink or possessions. It is also the title of a 1969 SciFi film staring Gregory Peck.

Landlubber

Someone without sailing ability. Not to be confused with Scallywag, which is what an experienced pirate would call a newbie.

Barbados and Piracy

Barbados is known for two pirates of the Caribbean - Sam Lord and Stede Bonnet.

Sam Lord

Sam Lord must have been one of the most unusual pirates of the caribbean! Rather than taking to the seas Sam Lord found a unique, and very profitable, way to plunder ships in the Caribbean.

Legend has it that Sam Lord hung lanterns in the coconut trees on the beach near his castle to attract ships. Captains mistook the lanterns for the lights of the capital city and wrecked their ships on the reefs.

Sam Lord, one of the most creative pirates of the Caribbean region, would then plunder the ships!

Stede Bonnet

Known as the pirate gentleman, Stede Bonnet became one of the pirates of the Caribbean in a most unusual way!

A retired British army major and well off plantation owner in Barbados, the middle aged Major Stede Bonnet suddenly turned to piracy in early 1717 and actually purchased his own pirate ship, an unheard of act among the pirates of the Caribbean!

Bonnet sailed his pirate ship "Revenge" off the New England Coast, capturing and burning several ships, before returning to the Caribbean. Here he befriended perhaps the most famous of the pirates of the Caribbean - Blackbeard!

These two pirates of the Caribbean led unusual lives and live on in legend even today...
